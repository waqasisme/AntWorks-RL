# `ty` is blocking in CI, overriding the original locked design

During 0.4 review, Claude flagged the CI workflow's blocking `ty check` step as contradicting the locked design's "beta → non-blocking" stance, and suggested `continue-on-error: true` plus removing the `# type: ignore` suppressions in `tests/test_config.py`. The user explicitly overrode this: `ty` stays blocking in CI, full stop — findings get fixed (or deliberately, visibly suppressed with a comment), never silently ignored via a non-blocking gate. `CLAUDE.md` and `0002-locked-design-decisions.md` updated accordingly; mypy/pyright remain the documented fallback if a `ty` beta gap ever proves genuinely unworkable.
